## 1. The Mindset of a Security Professional

Cyber security professionals often have access to sensitive systems and information.

Because of this access, they must behave responsibly and ethically.

A strong security mindset focuses on:
- protecting systems
- respecting rules
- thinking about consequences

---

## 2. Acting with Integrity

Integrity means doing the right thing even when no one is watching.

In cyber security, integrity includes:
- reporting vulnerabilities honestly
- following rules and policies
- refusing to misuse access privileges

Security professionals must be trusted by the organizations they work with.

---

## 3. Respecting Systems and Data

Professionals must respect the systems and data they work with.

Examples of responsible behavior:
- access only the data necessary for your work
- do not copy or share sensitive information
- do not attempt actions outside authorized scope
- respect the privacy and security of users

---

## 4. Thinking Before Acting

Security professionals should think carefully about consequences before taking action.

Before testing or research, ask:
- do I have permission to perform this action?
- is this within the approved scope?
- could this action harm systems or users?

Responsible decision-making helps prevent mistakes.

---

## 5. Building Trust in Cyber Security

Trust is essential in cyber security.

Organizations trust security professionals to:
- protect systems
- handle sensitive information responsibly
- follow ethical standards

Maintaining ethical behavior builds long-term trust and credibility.

---

## 6. Key Takeaways

- Cyber security professionals must act responsibly and ethically.
- Integrity and honesty are essential in security work.
- Sensitive systems and data must always be respected.
- Professionals should think carefully before taking action.
- Ethical behavior builds trust and credibility in cyber security.
