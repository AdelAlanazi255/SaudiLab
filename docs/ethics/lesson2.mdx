## 1. What is Hacking

Hacking generally means finding weaknesses in computer systems, software, or networks.

Hackers may try to:
- access systems
- discover vulnerabilities
- test security defenses

Hacking is not always illegal. What matters is how and why it is done.

---

## 2. Legal vs Illegal Hacking

Legal hacking happens when a person has permission to test a system.

Examples:
- security professionals hired to test systems
- penetration testers
- bug bounty researchers

Illegal hacking happens when someone accesses systems without permission.

Examples:
- stealing data
- breaking into accounts
- damaging systems

The key difference is authorization.

---

## 3. Types of Hackers (Hacker Hats)

### White Hat Hackers

White hat hackers are ethical hackers who work legally to improve security.

Examples:
- penetration testers
- security consultants
- bug bounty researchers

Their goal is to find and fix security weaknesses.

### Black Hat Hackers

Black hat hackers break into systems for harmful purposes.

Examples:
- stealing data
- spreading malware
- financial fraud

Their actions are illegal and harmful.

### Gray Hat Hackers

Gray hat hackers may access systems without permission but claim good intentions.

For example, they may find a vulnerability and report it later.

Even with good intent, accessing systems without permission can still be illegal.

---

## 4. Why Intent and Permission Matter

Cyber security professionals must always follow rules.

Even if someone has good intentions, accessing systems without permission can still break laws and policies.

Ethical hackers always:
- receive authorization
- follow clear testing rules
- respect system boundaries

---

## 5. The Risks of Illegal Hacking

Illegal hacking can lead to serious consequences.

Possible consequences include:
- legal penalties
- criminal charges
- financial fines
- damage to professional reputation

Cyber security professionals must always work within legal and ethical boundaries.

---

## 6. Key Takeaways

- Hacking means finding weaknesses in systems or software.
- Ethical hacking happens with permission and improves security.
- White hat hackers work legally to protect systems.
- Black hat hackers perform illegal and harmful activities.
- Accessing systems without permission can lead to serious consequences.
