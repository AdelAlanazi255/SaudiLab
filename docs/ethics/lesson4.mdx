## 1. Why Privacy Matters

Privacy means protecting personal information from being accessed or used without permission.

Many systems store sensitive data such as:
- personal messages
- phone numbers
- financial records
- medical information
- identification details

Cyber security professionals must respect and protect this information.

---

## 2. What is Consent

Consent means people agree before their data is collected, accessed, or used.

Organizations should clearly explain:
- what data they collect
- why they collect it
- how it will be used

Users should have control over their own information.

Accessing or using data without consent can violate privacy laws and ethical standards.

---

## 3. Handling Sensitive Data Responsibly

Security professionals may work with sensitive information.

Examples include:
- testing databases
- reviewing system logs
- analyzing security incidents

When working with sensitive data, professionals should:
- access only what is necessary
- avoid copying or sharing private data
- protect information from exposure
- follow company policies and legal requirements

---

## 4. Examples of Privacy Violations

Privacy can be violated in different ways.

Examples:
- accessing user data without authorization
- sharing personal information with unauthorized people
- collecting more data than necessary
- storing sensitive information without proper protection

These actions can damage trust and lead to legal consequences.

---

## 5. Respecting User Trust

Organizations and security professionals must protect user trust.

Users trust companies to protect their personal information.

Responsible cyber security professionals always act in ways that protect that trust.

Ethical behavior means thinking carefully about how actions affect other people.

---

## 6. Key Takeaways

- Privacy protects personal and sensitive information.
- Consent means users agree to how their data is collected and used.
- Security professionals must handle sensitive data responsibly.
- Violating privacy can harm individuals and organizations.
- Protecting user trust is a core responsibility in cyber security.
