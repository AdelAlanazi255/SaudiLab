## 1. What is Cyber Security

Cyber security is the practice of protecting digital systems, networks, and data from attacks.

Cyber attacks may try to:
- steal information
- damage systems
- disrupt services
- gain unauthorized access

Cyber security helps keep information and systems safe.

---

## 2. Why Cyber Security Matters

Modern life depends on digital systems every day.

Examples include:
- online banking
- email
- social media
- cloud storage
- online shopping
- government systems

If these systems are attacked, it can cause:
- financial loss
- stolen personal information
- service disruptions
- damage to businesses or organizations

---

## 3. What Needs Protection

Cyber security protects many important areas:

- Data: personal information, financial data, and business records.
- Devices: computers, smartphones, and servers.
- Networks: internet connections and internal company networks.
- Systems: websites, applications, and digital services.

Attackers often look for weaknesses in one or more of these areas.

---

## 4. Examples of Cyber Attacks

Some common cyber attacks are:

- Phishing: tricking people into sharing sensitive information.
- Malware: harmful software that can damage or control systems.
- Ransomware: malware that locks files and demands payment.
- Account hacking: unauthorized access to online accounts.

Later lessons will explain these attacks in more detail.

---

## 5. Who Is Responsible for Security

Cyber security is a shared responsibility.

- Organizations must secure their systems.
- Employees must follow security policies.
- Individuals must protect their personal accounts and devices.

Small mistakes can sometimes lead to big security problems.

---

## 6. Key Takeaways

- Cyber security protects digital systems and information.
- Many parts of modern life rely on secure technology.
- Cyber attacks can steal information or disrupt services.
- Both organizations and individuals play a role in security.
