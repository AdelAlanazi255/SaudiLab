## 1. The Goal of Cyber Security

Cyber security is not only about stopping hackers.

The main goal is to protect information and systems.

Security professionals often use a model called the **CIA Triad** to describe the core goals of cybersecurity.

The CIA Triad stands for:
- Confidentiality
- Integrity
- Availability

These three principles guide how systems and data are protected.

---

## 2. Confidentiality

Confidentiality means information should only be available to authorized people.

Examples:
- personal email should only be accessible to the account owner
- medical records should only be accessible to doctors and authorized staff
- bank account information should remain private

Attackers often try to break confidentiality by stealing sensitive information.

Common threats to confidentiality include:
- data breaches
- stolen passwords
- phishing attacks

---

## 3. Integrity

Integrity means information must remain accurate and trustworthy.

Data should not be changed or deleted by unauthorized users.

Example:
If someone changes the amount in a bank transaction, the data has lost integrity.

Organizations must protect important data from unauthorized changes.

Examples of threats to integrity:
- unauthorized modifications
- malware altering files
- attackers manipulating data

---

## 4. Availability

Availability means systems and data must be accessible when needed.

If systems are unavailable, users cannot access services or information.

Examples:
- a company website should remain accessible to customers
- hospital systems must be available so doctors can access patient information

Examples of threats to availability:
- system failures
- ransomware attacks
- distributed denial-of-service (DDoS) attacks

---

## 5. How the CIA Triad Works Together

Strong cybersecurity protects all three principles at the same time.

Examples:
- encryption helps protect confidentiality
- access controls help protect integrity
- reliable infrastructure helps maintain availability

If one principle fails, security can be compromised.

---

## 6. Key Takeaways

- The CIA Triad defines the core goals of cybersecurity.
- Confidentiality protects information from unauthorized access.
- Integrity ensures that data remains accurate and trustworthy.
- Availability ensures systems and information remain accessible.
- Cybersecurity systems aim to protect all three principles.
