## 1. What is a Cyber Attack

A cyber attack is an attempt to gain unauthorized access to systems, networks, or data.

Attackers may try to:
- steal sensitive information
- gain control of accounts
- disrupt services
- damage systems

Cyber attacks can target individuals, companies, and governments.

---

## 2. Phishing Attacks

Phishing is one of the most common cyber attacks.

Phishing attacks try to trick users into revealing sensitive information.

Examples:
- fake emails pretending to be from banks
- fake login pages that steal passwords
- messages asking users to verify their accounts

Attackers often create urgency to pressure victims into acting quickly.

Example:
"Your account will be locked if you do not reset your password immediately."

---

## 3. Password Attacks

Attackers often try to gain account access by targeting passwords.

Common password attack methods include:
- guessing weak passwords
- using leaked passwords from previous data breaches
- trying common passwords across many accounts

Password reuse makes these attacks much easier.

---

## 4. Social Engineering

Social engineering is when attackers manipulate people into revealing information or taking unsafe actions.

Instead of attacking systems directly, attackers trick people.

Examples:
- pretending to be technical support
- calling employees and asking for login information
- sending messages that appear to come from trusted contacts

---

## 5. Data Breaches

A data breach happens when sensitive information is exposed or stolen.

Examples of stolen information:
- usernames
- passwords
- credit card numbers
- personal information

Data breaches often happen when attackers gain access to company databases.

These breaches can affect millions of users.

---

## 6. Key Takeaways

- Cyber attacks attempt to gain unauthorized access to systems or information.
- Phishing is one of the most common attacks used to steal credentials.
- Weak passwords make accounts easier to compromise.
- Social engineering targets people rather than technology.
- Data breaches can expose large amounts of sensitive information.
