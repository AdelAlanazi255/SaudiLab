## 1. Why Data Needs Protection

When data travels across networks or is stored online, attackers may try to access it.

Sensitive data can include:
- passwords
- personal messages
- financial information
- business documents

Without protection, this information could be read or stolen.

---

## 2. What is Encryption

Encryption is the process of converting readable data into a coded format.

Only authorized users with the correct key or system can convert it back into readable form.

Example:

Normal message:
"Your bank balance is $500."

Encrypted version:
The information becomes unreadable to anyone without the proper key.

---

## 3. Encryption in Everyday Technology

Encryption is widely used in modern technology.

Examples include:
- secure websites (HTTPS)
- online banking systems
- encrypted messaging apps
- cloud storage services

When you see the lock icon in a browser address bar, it usually means the connection is encrypted.

---

## 4. How Encryption Protects Confidentiality

Encryption helps maintain **confidentiality**, one of the CIA Triad principles.

Even if attackers intercept encrypted data, they cannot easily understand it without the correct decryption key.

Encryption helps protect sensitive information during both transmission and storage.

---

## 5. When Encryption is Used

Encryption is used in many situations, including:
- sending data across the internet
- storing sensitive information in databases
- securing communication between devices
- protecting files on computers or smartphones

Encryption is an essential tool for protecting digital information.

---

## 6. Key Takeaways

- Encryption protects sensitive information by converting it into unreadable code.
- Only authorized users can decrypt encrypted data.
- Encryption is widely used in websites, messaging apps, and online services.
- Encryption helps protect confidentiality, one of the core goals of cybersecurity.
