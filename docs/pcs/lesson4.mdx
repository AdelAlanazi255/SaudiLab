## 1. Why Safe Browsing Matters

Every time you visit a website or download a file, you are interacting with unknown systems on the internet.

Some websites are safe, but others may contain scams, malware, or harmful downloads.

Unsafe browsing can lead to:
- stolen passwords
- malware infections
- financial scams
- identity theft

---

## 2. Risks When Browsing the Internet

Common beginner risks include:

- fake websites pretending to be real services
- malicious advertisements (malvertising)
- unsafe links sent through email or social media
- downloads that secretly install malware

Attackers often rely on tricks that make unsafe websites look normal.

---

## 3. Dangerous Downloads

Downloading files from untrusted sources can install harmful software.

Examples of risky downloads:
- cracked or pirated software
- unknown browser extensions
- fake software updates
- files from suspicious websites

Malware can hide inside files that seem harmless.

---

## 4. How to Identify Safe Websites

Use these simple checks before trusting a website:

- check the website address carefully
- look for HTTPS in the address bar
- avoid websites with strange domain names
- avoid clicking random pop-ups or ads

Attackers may copy real websites and change small details in the address.

Example:
`paypaI.com` (capital i instead of l)

---

## 5. Simple Safe Browsing Habits

Use these practical habits every day:

- only download software from official websites
- avoid clicking suspicious ads or pop-ups
- be cautious with browser extensions
- keep your browser and system updated
- use antivirus or security software if available

These small habits greatly reduce risk over time.

---

## 6. Key Takeaways

- Not all websites are safe.
- Malware often spreads through downloads.
- Fake websites can look very convincing.
- Safe browsing habits greatly reduce risk.
