## 1. What Phishing Is

Phishing is when attackers pretend to be a trusted organization to trick people into giving sensitive information.

They may try to steal:
- passwords
- banking details
- verification codes
- personal information

Phishing usually happens through:
- emails
- text messages
- fake websites
- social media messages

---

## 2. Why Phishing Works

Phishing works because attackers depend on human mistakes.

They often use simple tricks, such as:
- creating urgency
- pretending to be a trusted company
- sending messages that look official

Example:
"You must reset your password immediately or your account will be locked."

When people feel fear or pressure, they may click quickly without checking the message carefully.

---

## 3. Common Types of Phishing

- Email phishing: Fake bank or service emails asking you to log in.
- SMS phishing (smishing): Text messages pretending to be delivery companies or banks.
- Fake login pages: Websites that look real but are made to steal your login details.
- Social media scams: Messages pretending to be friends or support teams.

---

## 4. Warning Signs of a Phishing Message

Watch for these signs:
- strange or misspelled email addresses
- urgent or threatening messages
- links that look suspicious
- requests for passwords or verification codes

Always check who sent the message, and avoid clicking links if anything feels suspicious.

---

## 5. What To Do If You Receive One

If you receive a suspicious message:
- do not click suspicious links
- do not download attachments from unknown senders
- verify the message using the official website or app
- delete or report suspicious messages

Legitimate companies do not ask for your password by email or text.

---

## 6. Key Takeaways

- Phishing attacks try to trick people into revealing sensitive information.
- Attackers often pretend to be trusted organizations.
- Urgent messages are a common warning sign.
- Always verify suspicious messages before responding.
