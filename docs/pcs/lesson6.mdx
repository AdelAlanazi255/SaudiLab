## 1. Why Social Media Safety Matters

Social media platforms let people share photos, messages, and personal updates.

Common platforms include:
- Instagram
- Snapchat
- TikTok
- X (Twitter)
- Facebook
- LinkedIn

These platforms hold a lot of personal information, which makes them attractive targets for attackers and scammers.

---

## 2. Risks on Social Media Platforms

Common risks include:

- account hacking
- fake profiles pretending to be someone else
- scam messages asking for money
- malicious links sent through direct messages

Many attacks on social media rely on tricking people, not advanced technical methods.

---

## 3. Oversharing Personal Information

Sharing too much personal information online can increase security risks.

Examples of risky information:
- home address
- phone number
- travel plans
- personal documents
- photos showing sensitive details

Attackers may use this information to guess passwords, answer security questions, or impersonate someone.

---

## 4. Protecting Your Social Media Accounts

Use these simple safety practices:

- use strong and unique passwords
- enable two-factor authentication when available
- review privacy settings on your accounts
- avoid accepting requests from unknown people

Privacy settings help control who can see your personal information.

---

## 5. Recognizing Social Media Scams

Common scams include:

- messages pretending to be friends asking for money
- fake giveaways asking you to click links
- links promising free rewards or prizes

Scammers often create urgency or excitement to push people into quick decisions.

Always verify suspicious messages before you respond.

---

## 6. Key Takeaways

- Social media accounts contain valuable personal information.
- Oversharing online can increase security risks.
- Strong passwords and two-factor authentication help protect accounts.
- Be cautious when receiving messages from unknown users.
- Always verify suspicious links and requests.
