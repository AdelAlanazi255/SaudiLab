## 1. What Public WiFi Is

Public WiFi is internet access provided in public places.

Common examples include:
- cafes
- airports
- hotels
- shopping malls
- universities

These networks allow many people to connect at the same time.

---

## 2. Why Public WiFi Can Be Risky

Public WiFi networks are often less secure than private home networks.

Because many people share the same network, attackers may try to monitor activity or trick users.

Possible risks include:
- attackers spying on network traffic
- fake WiFi networks
- stolen login information

Not all public WiFi is dangerous, but it is important to stay cautious.

---

## 3. Common Public WiFi Threats

- Fake WiFi hotspots: Attackers create network names that look like real ones.
  Example: `FreeCafeWiFi` vs `Free_Cafe_WiFi`
- Unsecured networks: Some networks do not require passwords, so they are easier to monitor.
- Man-in-the-middle attacks: An attacker secretly stands between you and a website to intercept information.

---

## 4. Protecting Your Devices

Use simple actions to keep your device safer:

- use a screen lock on your phone and laptop
- keep your operating system updated
- avoid logging into sensitive accounts on unknown networks
- disable automatic WiFi connections when possible

Updates are important because they fix known security weaknesses.

---

## 5. Safe Public WiFi Habits

Use these habits whenever you connect to public WiFi:

- avoid banking or other sensitive accounts on public WiFi
- use secure websites with HTTPS
- turn off file sharing on public networks
- disconnect from WiFi when finished

Small habits like these can greatly reduce risk.

---

## 6. Key Takeaways

- Public WiFi is convenient but can introduce risks.
- Attackers may create fake WiFi networks.
- Devices should always be updated and protected.
- Avoid accessing sensitive accounts on public networks.
- Safe habits greatly reduce exposure.
