## Introduction

Blue Teams cannot protect systems if they do not know what is happening inside them.

To detect threats, organizations monitor system activity and review logs.

Logs and monitoring help security teams identify suspicious behavior early.

## What Monitoring Means

Monitoring means watching systems and networks for unusual or suspicious activity.

Security teams monitor many things, such as:
- login attempts
- system changes
- network connections
- application activity

Monitoring helps detect attacks before they cause serious damage.

## What Logs Are

Logs are records created by systems that track events and activity.

Many systems automatically record logs to document what happens.

Examples of logged activity include:
- user logins
- file access
- system errors
- network connections

Logs provide a historical record of what occurred on a system.

## How Blue Teams Use Logs

Blue Team analysts review logs to answer questions such as:
- Who logged into the system?
- When did the login occur?
- What actions were performed?
- Was the activity normal or suspicious?

Logs help analysts detect security incidents and investigate problems.

## Example Scenario

A company notices unusual system activity late at night.

When security analysts review the logs, they discover:
- multiple failed login attempts
- a successful login from an unusual location
- sensitive files accessed shortly afterward

This information helps the Blue Team investigate a potential attack.

## Why Monitoring Is Critical

Without monitoring and logs:
- attacks may go unnoticed
- suspicious behavior may be ignored
- investigations become difficult

Logs and monitoring give Blue Teams visibility into system activity.

## Key Takeaways

- Monitoring helps detect suspicious activity.
- Logs record system events and actions.
- Blue Teams review logs to investigate security incidents.
- Logs provide valuable evidence during investigations.
