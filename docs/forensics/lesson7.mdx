## Introduction

Digital forensics investigators often reconstruct incidents by analyzing evidence and digital traces.

This lesson walks through a fictional investigation example to show how investigators apply the concepts learned in this course.

## The Scenario

A company notices that confidential files were downloaded from an internal system late at night.

The security team suspects that someone accessed sensitive information without authorization.

A digital forensics investigation begins to determine what happened.

## Identifying the Evidence

Investigators look for possible evidence sources, such as:
- system login records
- file access logs
- employee computer activity
- network connection logs

Investigators first identify where relevant digital traces may exist.

## Reviewing Logs

System logs reveal that a user account logged into the system at 2:17 AM, outside normal working hours.

The logs show that several sensitive files were accessed shortly after the login.

This information helps investigators understand when the activity occurred.

## Analyzing File Evidence

Metadata from the files shows:
- when the files were accessed
- which account accessed them

Investigators also calculate file hashes to confirm that the files have not been modified during analysis.

## Reconstructing the Timeline

Investigators combine all evidence to build a timeline:

2:17 AM - User account logs into the system  
2:20 AM - Sensitive files are accessed  
2:25 AM - Files are copied to a USB device

This helps investigators understand how the incident occurred.

## Investigation Conclusion

Based on logs, metadata, and activity records, the team determines:
- which account accessed the system
- when the activity occurred
- which files were involved

The findings are documented and reported to management for further action.

## Key Takeaways

- Digital forensics investigations combine multiple evidence sources.
- Logs, metadata, and system records help reconstruct events.
- Investigators build timelines to understand incidents.
- Evidence must be preserved and documented carefully.
