## Introduction

Digital investigations follow a structured process.

Investigators do not randomly analyze devices.

Instead, they follow a series of steps to ensure evidence is handled correctly and conclusions are reliable.

This structured approach helps maintain accuracy and credibility.

## Step 1: Evidence Identification

Investigators first determine what evidence may exist.

Examples include:
- computers
- smartphones
- servers
- USB drives
- network logs
- email records

The goal is to identify all possible sources of digital evidence.

## Step 2: Evidence Collection

Investigators collect devices or digital copies of evidence while preserving the original data.

Important principles include:
- avoiding modification of evidence
- documenting how evidence was collected
- maintaining chain of custody

Careful collection ensures the evidence remains reliable.

## Step 3: Evidence Analysis

Investigators analyze the collected data to understand what happened.

They may examine:
- system logs
- file activity
- login records
- network traces
- timestamps

This stage helps investigators reconstruct events.

## Step 4: Documentation & Reporting

Investigators must document their findings.

Documentation includes:
- how evidence was collected
- what was discovered during analysis
- the timeline of events
- conclusions based on the evidence

Clear reporting allows others to understand the investigation results.

## Why a Structured Process Matters

Following a structured investigation process helps ensure:
- evidence remains trustworthy
- investigations remain organized
- conclusions are supported by data
- findings can be explained clearly

This process is especially important in legal or corporate investigations.

## Key Takeaways

- Digital forensics investigations follow a structured process.
- Investigators identify, collect, analyze, and document evidence.
- Proper evidence handling ensures reliable results.
- Clear documentation helps explain investigation findings.
