## Introduction

Digital forensics is the process of investigating digital devices and systems.

Investigators look for evidence on computers, phones, servers, and networks.  
The goal is to understand what happened during an incident.

Digital forensics can be used in situations like:
- investigating cybercrime
- tracking a data breach
- analyzing suspicious employee activity
- examining hacked systems

## Where Digital Forensics Is Used

Digital forensics is used in many areas:

Law enforcement:
- investigating cybercrime
- analyzing seized computers

Companies:
- investigating internal security incidents
- understanding how breaches happened

Cybersecurity teams:
- analyzing attacks
- identifying attacker actions

## Types of Digital Evidence

Digital evidence can come from many places, including:
- files on computers
- emails and messages
- system logs
- browser history
- USB device activity
- network records

Even deleted activity can sometimes be recovered.

## The Role of a Digital Forensics Analyst

A digital forensics analyst works carefully to find and explain evidence.

Typical responsibilities include:
- collecting evidence safely
- analyzing digital data
- reconstructing events
- documenting findings
- presenting evidence clearly

Accuracy and careful handling are extremely important.

## Why Digital Forensics Matters

Digital forensics is important because it:
- helps identify attackers
- helps explain how attacks happened
- helps organizations improve security
- provides evidence in legal investigations

## Key Takeaways

- Digital forensics investigates digital devices and systems.
- Investigators analyze data to understand security incidents.
- Evidence can come from files, logs, messages, and system activity.
- Digital forensics helps organizations respond to cyber incidents.
