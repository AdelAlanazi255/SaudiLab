## Introduction

Digital investigations depend on evidence.

Evidence helps investigators answer questions such as:
- What happened?
- When did it happen?
- Who was involved?

Digital evidence must be handled very carefully so it can be trusted.

## What Digital Evidence Is

Digital evidence is information stored on electronic devices that can help explain an event.

Examples of digital evidence include:
- files on a computer
- emails or chat messages
- browser history
- login records
- USB device activity
- network logs

Even small traces of activity can become important evidence.

## Why Evidence Must Be Protected

Investigators must protect evidence from changes.

If evidence is changed accidentally:
- it may no longer be trusted
- it may become unusable in investigations
- it may not be accepted in legal cases

Because of this, investigators follow strict procedures when handling evidence.

## What Chain of Custody Means

Chain of custody means a record of who collected, handled, and accessed evidence from the moment it was discovered.

It answers questions like:
- Who found the evidence?
- When was it collected?
- Who analyzed it?
- Where was it stored?

This ensures the evidence remains trustworthy.

## Simple Example

A company computer is suspected of being used to steal data.

The investigator:
1. Documents the computer location
2. Records the time it was collected
3. Safely stores the device
4. Records every person who accesses it

This documentation creates the chain of custody.

## Why Chain of Custody Matters

Without chain of custody:
- evidence can be questioned
- investigations become unreliable
- legal cases may fail

Proper evidence handling ensures investigations remain credible and professional.

## Key Takeaways

- Digital evidence is data stored on electronic devices.
- Evidence must be handled carefully to prevent changes.
- Chain of custody tracks who handled evidence and when.
- Proper documentation protects the integrity of investigations.
